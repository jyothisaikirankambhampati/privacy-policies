WireWarden

Privacy Policy  ·  Effective Date: May 25, 2025

NO DATA COLLECTED · NO DATA SHARED

1. Overview

WireWarden is a local, on-device VPN firewall. It does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data with the developer or any third party. All processing happens entirely on your device.

This Privacy Policy describes how WireWarden ("the App"), developed by Jyothi Sai Kiran Kambhampati, handles information in connection with your use of the App on Android devices. By installing or using WireWarden you acknowledge this policy.

2. How the VPN Is Used

WireWarden uses Android's VpnService API to create a local VPN tunnel entirely on your device. This tunnel intercepts network packets originating from installed apps and either forwards them (allowed apps) or drops them (blocked apps) based on rules you configure.

The VPN traffic never leaves your device. WireWarden does not act as a proxy, does not route your traffic through any remote server, and does not inspect packet payloads. The sole purpose of the VPN is to enforce per-app firewall rules locally.

3. Data We Collect

WireWarden collects no data. Specifically:

  • We do not collect personal information (name, email, location, etc.).
  • We do not log or inspect the content of any network traffic.
  • We do not collect usage statistics, analytics, or crash reports.
  • We do not use advertising SDKs or tracking libraries.
  • We do not access your contacts, camera, microphone, or files.

4. Data Stored on Your Device

WireWarden stores the following data locally on your device only, using Android's Room database and SharedPreferences:

What is stored Why Where
Per-app firewall rules (WiFi / Mobile allowed or blocked) To enforce your chosen rules across sessions and after reboot Local SQLite database (Room)
Named firewall profiles To let you save and switch between rule sets Local SQLite database (Room)
VPN on/off state, battery prompt shown flag App preferences between sessions SharedPreferences (local)

This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly use the Backup / Export feature to save a JSON file to a location you choose.

Additionally, WireWarden uses Android's TrafficStats API to read the cumulative bytes sent and received by each installed app (RX + TX totals). This information is displayed in the app list as a live data-usage indicator and is never stored persistently, never transmitted, and never shared. It exists only in memory while the app is open and is discarded when the app is closed.

5. Permissions Explained

Permission Purpose
BIND_VPN_SERVICE Required to create the local VPN tunnel for per-app traffic control
FOREGROUND_SERVICE / FOREGROUND_SERVICE_SPECIAL_USE Keeps the VPN firewall active while the app is in the background
RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED Automatically restarts the firewall after device reboot if it was active
ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE Detects whether the device is on WiFi or mobile data to apply the correct rule
INTERNET Required by the VpnService API; no internet connections are made by the app itself
QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES Reads the list of installed apps so you can set per-app firewall rules
KILL_BACKGROUND_PROCESSES Terminates background processes of newly-blocked apps so rules take effect immediately
REQUEST_IGNORE_BATTERY_OPTIMIZATIONS Prompts you to exempt WireWarden from battery saver, preventing the OS from stopping the firewall
BIND_QUICK_SETTINGS_TILE Enables the Quick Settings tile for toggling the firewall from the notification shade

6. Third-Party Services

WireWarden does not integrate any third-party SDKs, analytics services, advertising networks, or cloud backends. The app has no network activity of its own.

7. Children's Privacy

WireWarden is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children. Because the app collects no data at all, there is no special risk to children's privacy.

8. Security

Because WireWarden does not transmit data to any server, there is no remote attack surface for your personal data. Firewall rules are stored in the app's private data partition, inaccessible to other apps without root access.

9. GDPR & CCPA Compliance

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) — EU/EEA users

WireWarden does not collect, process, or transfer any personal data as defined under the GDPR. No lawful basis for processing is required because no personal data is processed. There are no data subjects to notify, no data processors involved, and no cross-border data transfers. As a result, WireWarden is fully compliant with GDPR by design.

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) — California residents

WireWarden does not sell, share, or disclose personal information to any third party. California residents have the right to know what personal information is collected — the answer is none. No opt-out mechanism is required because there is nothing to opt out of. WireWarden does not discriminate against users who exercise their privacy rights.
